Trekking


Trekking is a sort of walking that is done to explore and appreciate nature. It generally happens on trails in largely untouched mountain regions.
The world's major mountain ranges are among the most picturesque and fascinating places to explore while trekking.
They might be the most distant and challenging locations to go to because they are usually not accessible by road, and the only viable way to visit them is by walking.
Trekking may be an aim in itself for some, a means to appreciate the breathtaking views for others, and the inhabitants of the mountains, with their culture, traditions,
and religions, frequently add an equal amount of appeal to the natural beauty.
There is no dearth of trekking in Uttarakhand. The state is home to the mighty Himalayas and offers treks for all kinds of enthusiasts.

The best time to visit Uttarakhand for trekking is in the summer months of May-June.

Experience Natural Beauty


A land of myths and mountains, Pauri, is one of the most beautiful place of India. Snow-capped Himalayan peaks, serpentine rivers, revered temples, quaint villages, vibrant culture, and World Heritage Sites, Pauri's sheer natural beauty and diversity beckon travelers from across the world.


Khirsu


The picturesque hill station located in the Pauri Garhwal district offers spectacular views of the Himalayas,
including a panorama of peaks like the Panchachuli, Nanda Devi, Nanda Kot, and Trishul. Perched at a height of 1,700 m, Khirsu is dotted
with dense pine and deodar forests, which make for excellent venues for nature walks and hikes. The hamlet is endowed with lush orchards of apples and wildflowers of every kind.
The village is also an agricultural hub and surrunding it are picturesque terrace farms.


Devprayag


Devprayag is the place where the holy rivers Bhagirathi and Alaknanda meet, merge into one and take the name Ganga,
making it a unique pilgrimage. It is believed that Lord Rama and his father King Dasharath (from the epic Ramayana) performed penance here.
The main temple of the town is Raghunathji Temple and is dedicated to Lord Rama. Devprayag is one of the Panch Prayag or five sacred confluences of the Alaknanda river.
The rivers converge to flow down to the plains as River Ganga. The five holy sites are Vishnuprayag, Nandaprayag, Karnaprayag, Rudraprayag and Devprayag.
The town is also the winter seat for the priests of Badrinath Dham temple.


Lansdowne


Lansdowne is an unspoiled expanse of lush greenery nestled in the Himalayas. The hill station is dotted with pines and oaks
and is perched at a height of 1,700 m. Charming churches, flowering thickets, springs, and ponds encompass the entire hill station.
Lansdowne was established in 1887 as a cantonment of the British, who were drawn to its salubrious climate, and is named after the then Viceroy of India, Lord Lansdowne.
Today, it is the headquarters of the Garhwal Rifles of the Indian Army. Explore the small town on foot, with a lake at its centre, enjoy nature walks and discover
hidden temples and churches or just laze around and enjoy the pristine mountain views.

Kandoliya Devta Temple


Another Shiva temple (Kandolia Devta) is located in the dense forest of Oak and Pine on Kandolia hills.
Adjacent to this temple is a beautiful park, sports complex and a few mts ahead is Asia’s highest Stadium Ransi. During summers Kandolia Park
Witnesses the cheering, laughing and funfilled families from the locale. One side of the park gives a beautiful view of Pauri town and other side of it gives a beautiful view of Gangwarsyun valley.
The place is one kilometre ascend on foot from bus stand or 2km from motorable road.
Professional or amateur photographer can capture a lot in their camera to cherish.


Kyunkaleshwar Temple


Kyunkaleshwar Temple 8th century Shiva temple established by Sankaracharya during his visit to Pauri with the view of, revival of Hinduism.
The temple is well renowned in Pauri and nearby areas, people has a very strong belief in the temple with main Deities-
Lord Shiva, Parvati, Ganesha, Kartikeya. Other Deities in the temple just behind the main temple are of Lord Rama, Lakshmana & Goddess Sita.
One can have a spellbound sight of the magnificent Himalayan ranges along with Alaknanda Valley and town itself from here.


Jwalpa Devi Temple


Situated at the panorama of the hills near the river, Jvalpa Devi temple has
the peaceful environment in its premises. This Temple was constructed by Late Dutta Ram Anthwal,
father of Late Butha Ram Anthwal. Now, Anthwal Samiti and Jvalpa Devi Samiti take care of the temple.
Anthwals are the pujaris (priests), here since the temple was constructed. They offer a special puja on the occasion of Navaratri. /p>




how to reach

By Flight

The nearest Airport to haldwani is Jollygrant Airport, Dehradun. From Dehradun, you can take a bus to reach Pauri. The distance between Dehradun to Pauri is 71 kms. The next nearest airport is Chandigarh Airport i.e. 200 kms away.

By Road

NH-119 is a well-maintained road path by which you can make your reliable journey here. You can take a bus through NH-119 to Kotdwar or Pauri.
